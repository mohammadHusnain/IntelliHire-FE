The project is an AI-based interview, recruitment, and talent community platform that serves two main types of users: individual users and companies. The purpose of the system is to help individuals prepare for interviews through mock interview practice, while also helping companies manage hiring in a smarter and more automated way. In addition to interview and hiring support, the system also allows companies to build targeted communities of students, alumni, or specific talent groups for future opportunities.
For individual users, the system works as a mock interview platform. A user can sign up, create a profile, upload a CV, and choose a target role or interview type. Based on that, the system can conduct an AI-powered mock interview, either general or role-specific, and then provide feedback. This feedback can include communication quality, confidence, technical relevance, strengths, weak areas, and suggestions for improvement. The goal on the individual side is interview preparation and self-improvement.
For companies, the system works as a recruitment and screening platform. A company can onboard into the system, access its own dashboard, and create job openings. When creating a job opening, the company can define the role, required skills, experience, and any custom fields it wants from applicants. Based on this, the system generates an application form. The company can then share this form link externally on platforms like LinkedIn, social media, university groups, or any other channel. Interested candidates can open that form, submit their information, upload their CV, and apply for the job. All submitted data is automatically saved in our database.
After candidates apply, the system starts the hiring pipeline. Once the application deadline is reached, or once the company decides to begin screening, the system analyzes the candidate data. This includes CV analysis, where the system extracts useful information such as skills, education, projects, and experience, and compares it with the job requirements. Based on this analysis, the system shortlists the more relevant candidates. These shortlisted candidates then move to the next stage, which is the AI screening interview. This screening interview is used for initial filtering and can assess things like communication, motivation, basic understanding of the role, and overall suitability.
After the screening stage, the stronger candidates move to the technical interview stage. In this stage, the system conducts a more role-focused AI interview based on the job description, required skills, and possibly the candidate’s own CV content. The technical interview is intended to evaluate job-fit in more depth. At the end of these interview stages, the system generates a structured evaluation report for each candidate. This report can include CV match score, screening performance, technical performance, strengths, weaknesses, and an overall recommendation. These results are shown on the company’s HR or recruiter dashboard so that human recruiters can review the shortlisted candidates and make the final decision.
Another important part of the project is the community feature. Companies can create communities inside the system to connect with specific groups of users, such as students from a certain university, alumni from a department, or users with particular skills or interests. This feature is not meant to be a full social media platform, but rather a focused talent network. Through these communities, companies can build long-term relationships with potential candidates, share opportunities with specific groups, and maintain a filtered talent pool for future hiring.
So overall, the system combines three connected ideas in one platform. First, it provides mock interview practice for solo users. Second, it gives companies a complete AI-assisted hiring workflow, starting from job form creation and application collection all the way to candidate screening and reporting. Third, it supports alumni, student, and company communities so that hiring is not only about one-time job posts, but also about building long-term talent connections. The system is designed to assist decision-making through AI, but the final hiring decision still remains with the company, making it an AI-assisted recruitment platform rather than a fully automated replacement for recruiters.
